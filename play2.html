SCENE II.--HARPAGON, THE POLICE OFFICER, MASTER JACQUES.

JAC.

(_at the end of the stage, turning back to the door by which he came in_). I am coming back. Have his throat cut at once; have his feet singed; put him in boiling water, and hang him up to the ceiling.

HAR.

What! Him who has robbed me?

JAC.

I was speaking of a sucking pig that your steward has just sent me; and I want to have it dressed for you after my own fancy.

HAR.

This is no longer the question; and you have to speak of something else to this gentleman.

OFF.

(_to_ JACQUES). Don't get frightened. I am not a man to cause any scandal, and matters will be carried on by gentle means.
